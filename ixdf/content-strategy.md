# Content Strategy

## Summary
Content strategy focuses on the planning, creation, delivery, and governance of content. It ensures that the right content is delivered to the right user at the right time for the right purpose. Content strategy is a crucial component of UX design that deals with the substance of what users interact with.

## Core Concepts
- Content strategy deals with the creation, publication, and governance of useful, usable content
- It focuses on the substance of what users interact with, not just the presentation
- Good content strategy aligns content with business goals and user needs
- It encompasses everything from microcopy to long-form content

## Key Components of Content Strategy
1. **Substance**: What content needs to be created and why
   - Content types and topics
   - Messaging architecture
   - Content requirements and sources

2. **Structure**: How content is organized and prioritized
   - Information architecture integration
   - Metadata and taxonomy
   - Content modeling

3. **Workflow**: How content gets created and maintained
   - Roles and responsibilities
   - Tools and technologies
   - Processes and standards

4. **Governance**: How decisions about content are made
   - Policies and guidelines
   - Standards and compliance
   - Maintenance and evolution

## Content Strategy Process
1. **Analysis**: Auditing existing content and understanding user needs
2. **Planning**: Developing a roadmap for content creation and maintenance
3. **Creation**: Producing or sourcing content according to strategy
4. **Implementation**: Publishing and integrating content into the experience
5. **Evaluation**: Measuring effectiveness and refining based on results

## Content Strategy Deliverables
- Content audits and inventories
- Content models and matrices
- Editorial calendars and workflows
- Style guides and voice/tone documentation
- Governance frameworks and policies
- Content management system specifications
