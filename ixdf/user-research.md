# User Research

## Summary
User research is the systematic investigation of users and their requirements, to add context and insight into the design process. It helps designers understand user behaviors, needs, and motivations through various qualitative and quantitative methods.

## Core Concepts
- User research informs design decisions with real user data
- It reduces risk by validating assumptions before implementation
- Research can be generative (exploratory) or evaluative (testing)
- Methods range from interviews and surveys to usability testing and analytics

## Types of User Research
### Qualitative Research
- **Interviews**: One-on-one conversations to explore user experiences
- **Ethnographic Studies**: Observing users in their natural environment
- **Focus Groups**: Guided discussions with multiple participants
- **Diary Studies**: Users record their experiences over time

### Quantitative Research
- **Surveys**: Collecting standardized data from many users
- **Analytics**: Analyzing behavioral data from existing products
- **A/B Testing**: Comparing two versions to determine which performs better
- **Tree Testing**: Evaluating information architecture findability

## When to Conduct User Research
1. **Discovery Phase**: Understanding the problem space and user needs
2. **Design Phase**: Testing concepts and iterating on designs
3. **Validation Phase**: Evaluating designs with real users
4. **Post-Launch**: Monitoring usage and identifying improvement areas

## Key User Research Methods
- **User Interviews**: Deep dives into user experiences and motivations
- **Surveys**: Broad data collection on user preferences and behaviors
- **Usability Testing**: Observing users interact with prototypes or products
- **Card Sorting**: Understanding how users categorize information
- **Journey Mapping**: Visualizing the end-to-end user experience
- **Persona Development**: Creating archetypal user profiles based on research

## Best Practices
- Define clear research goals before starting
- Recruit participants that represent your target audience
- Use a mix of methods for triangulation
- Synthesize findings into actionable insights
- Share research results with stakeholders and team members
- Continuously iterate based on new research findings
