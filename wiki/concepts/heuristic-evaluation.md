# Heuristic Evaluations: How to Conduct

## Summary
Step-by-step instructions to systematically review a product to find potential usability and experience problems by judging the design against a set of guidelines (heuristics).

## Key Takeaways
- Heuristic evaluations identify design problems by comparing interfaces against established guidelines
- Jakob Nielsen's 10 usability heuristics are recommended for general usability assessments
- The method works best with 3-5 evaluators working independently to catch different issues
- Heuristic evaluations complement (but don't replace) user research with actual participants
- The process involves preparation, independent evaluation, consolidation of findings, and exception handling

## Origins/History
Developed as a usability inspection method to evaluate interfaces without requiring user participation. Based on Jakob Nielsen's 1994 article "The Theory Behind Heuristic Evaluations" and earlier work with Rolf Molich in 1990.

## Applications
Heuristic evaluations are applied to:
- Identify glaring problems in interfaces early in the design process
- Stretch limited UX research budgets by finding likely issues without participants
- Develop UX instincts through practice evaluating various products
- Complement user research by identifying elements to target during testing
- Train new UX professionals to catch common usability issues
