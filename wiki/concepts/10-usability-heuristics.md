# 10 Usability Heuristics for User Interface Design

## Summary
Jakob Nielsen's 10 general principles for interaction design that serve as broad rules of thumb for evaluating and improving user interfaces. These heuristics have remained relevant for over 30 years and provide foundational guidance for usability engineering.

## Key Takeaways
- The 10 heuristics cover visibility, real-world matching, user control, consistency, error prevention, recognition, flexibility, aesthetic design, error handling, and help/documentation
- They are "heuristics" - broad rules of thumb rather than specific guidelines
- Originally developed in 1990 and refined in 1994, they remain largely unchanged
- Serve as the foundation for heuristic evaluation methods
- Applicable across platforms and interface types

## Origins/History
Developed by Jakob Nielsen in collaboration with Rolf Molich in 1990 for heuristic evaluation. Refined in 1994 based on factor analysis of 249 usability problems to maximize explanatory power. Updated in 2020 with additional explanation while keeping the core heuristics unchanged.

## Applications
The 10 Usability Heuristics are applied to:
- Conduct heuristic evaluations of interfaces
- Guide design decisions during development
- Train designers in usability principles
- Evaluate existing systems for usability issues
- Create usability guidelines and style guides
- Inform usability testing protocols
