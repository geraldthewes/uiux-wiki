# Cards in Material Design 3

## Summary
Material Design 3 cards are versatile containers that display content and actions related to a single subject. They provide a flexible way to present information with consistent visual treatment, appropriate spacing, and clear interaction patterns while maintaining accessibility and responsiveness.

## Card Anatomy
- **Container**: Surface with rounded corners (typically 8dp radius) and elevation for depth
- **Content Areas**: Header (optional), Media (optional), Primary Content, Actions (optional)
- **Dividers**: Optional thin lines (1dp) to separate sections within the card

## Card Types
- **Outline Card**: Visible border with transparent background, subtle grouping
- **Filled Card**: Solid background color (surface variant), most common type
- **Elevated Card**: Increased elevation for pronounced depth, used for dialogs/popovers

## Usage Guidelines
- **Content Organization**: One subject per card, progressive disclosure, consistent alignment
- **Interaction Patterns**: Tap (entire card), Swipe (dismissal/reveal), Drag (reordering), Expand (detailed content)
- **Accessibility**: Minimum 48x48dp touch target, clear focus indicators, sufficient contrast
- **Responsive Behavior**: Reflow in grids, adaptive layouts, horizontal scrolling on narrow screens

## Applications
- Displaying user profiles with avatars and metadata
- Presenting product information with images and descriptions
- Showing news articles with headlines and summaries
- Organizing settings options with toggles and controls
- Creating dashboards with metrics and visualizations
- Building interactive lists with swipe-to-dismiss or reorder
