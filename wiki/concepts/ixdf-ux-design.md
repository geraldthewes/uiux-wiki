# User Experience (UX) Design

## Summary
User experience (UX) design is the process design teams use to create products that provide meaningful and relevant experiences to users. UX design involves the design of the entire process of acquiring and integrating the product, including aspects of branding, design, usability and function.

## Core Concepts
- UX design focuses on the entire user journey, not just the interface
- It encompasses usability, accessibility, pleasure, efficiency and fun
- UX designers advocate for users throughout the design and development process
- The goal is to solve real user problems in specific contexts

## UX vs UI Design
While often confused, UX and UI design are distinct but complementary disciplines:
- **UX Design**: Focuses on the overall feel of the experience, the entire process of acquiring and integrating a product
- **UI Design**: Focuses on how the product is laid out, the visual elements and interactive components

## Key Principles
1. **User-Centered Design**: Starting with understanding users and their context
2. **Iterative Process**: Continuously refining designs based on user feedback
3. **Holistic Approach**: Considering all touchpoints in the user journey
4. **Accessibility**: Designing for diverse user abilities and needs
5. **Usability**: Ensuring products are easy to learn and efficient to use

## UX Designer Responsibilities
- Conducting user research and creating personas
- Designing wireframes and interactive prototypes
- Performing usability testing
- Collaborating with developers and stakeholders
- Advocating for user needs throughout the product lifecycle
