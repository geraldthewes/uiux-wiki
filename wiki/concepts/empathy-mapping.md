# Empathy Mapping: The First Step in Design Thinking

## Summary
Visualizing user attitudes and behaviors in an empathy map helps UX teams align on a deep understanding of end users. The mapping process also reveals any holes in existing user data.

## Key Takeaways
- Empathy maps externalize knowledge about users into four quadrants: Says, Thinks, Does, and Feels
- They create shared understanding of user needs and aid in decision making
- Empathy maps can be individual (one user) or aggregated (user segments)
- The process helps discover gaps in current knowledge and identify needed research
- They serve as a starting point for creating personas and removing bias from designs

## Origins/History
Developed as a tool in user-centered design and design thinking processes. Popularized by NN/g and design thinking practitioners as a way to visualize qualitative research findings and build team alignment around user understanding.

## Applications
Empathy mapping is applied by UX teams to:
- Establish common ground among team members about users
- Visualize and organize qualitative research findings
- Discover weaknesses in current user research
- Align on user needs to prioritize design efforts
- Create starting points for persona development
- Guide innovation by uncovering unspoken user motivations
- Remove bias from designs by grounding decisions in user data
