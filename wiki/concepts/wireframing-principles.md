# Wireframing Principles
## Summary

Wireframing principles are foundational guidelines that ensure wireframes effectively communicate interface structure, functionality, and user flows without unnecessary visual distractions. These principles help designers focus on what matters most in the early stages of design: solving user problems through thoughtful layout and interaction design.

## Key Takeaways
- **Simplicity**: Strip away non-essential elements to focus on core structure and functionality
- **User-Centricity**: Design wireframes based on user needs, goals, and mental models
- **Clarity**: Ensure every element in the wireframe serves a clear purpose and is easily understandable
- **Feedback Loops**: Use wireframes to gather early feedback from users and stakeholders
- **Consistency**: Apply consistent conventions and patterns across wireframes for predictability
- **Annotation**: Use notes to explain interactions, behaviors, and edge cases that aren't visually apparent
- **Fidelity Appropriateness**: Match wireframe fidelity to the current design goals and audience needs

## Origins/History
Wireframing principles emerged from architectural drafting and engineering practices, adapted for digital interface design as the need to plan complex interactions grew with the advent of graphical user interfaces and websites.

## Applications
- Evaluating the effectiveness of wireframes during design reviews
- Guiding designers in creating purposeful low-fidelity representations
- Ensuring wireframes serve their intended communication function
- Establishing team standards for wireframe creation and review
- Teaching wireframing fundamentals to new designers
