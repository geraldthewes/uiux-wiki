# Cards in Material Design 3

## Summary
Material Design 3 cards are versatile containers that display content and actions related to a single subject. They provide a flexible way to present information with consistent visual treatment, appropriate spacing, and clear interaction patterns while maintaining accessibility and responsiveness.

## Key Takeaways
- **Card Anatomy**: Container (surface with rounded corners, elevation), Content Areas (Header, Media, Primary Content, Actions), Dividers (optional thin lines)
- **Card Types**: Outline Card (visible border, transparent background), Filled Card (solid background color), Elevated Card (increased elevation)
- **Usage Guidelines**: One subject per card, progressive disclosure, consistent alignment and spacing, appropriate typographic hierarchy
- **Interaction Patterns**: Tap (entire card), Swipe (dismissal/revelation), Drag (long press and drag for reordering), Expand (tap to reveal more content)
- **Accessibility**: Minimum touch target: 48x48dp, clear focus indicators, sufficient contrast (4.5:1 normal), accessible labels for icon-only actions
- **Responsive Behavior**: Reflow in grids based on available width, consistent height for grid alignment, horizontal scrolling on narrow screens, adaptive layouts

## Origins/History
Material Design 3 was introduced by Google in 2021 as the next evolution of Material Design, focusing on flexibility, personalization, and cross-platform consistency.

## Applications
- Displaying user profiles with avatars and metadata
- Presenting product information with images and descriptions
- Showing news articles with headlines and summaries
- Organizing settings options with toggles and controls
- Creating dashboards with metrics and visualizations
- Building interactive lists with swipe-to-dismiss or reorder
