# Nielsen Norman Group: Empathy Mapping

## Summary
Visualizing user attitudes and behaviors in an empathy map helps UX teams align on a deep understanding of end users. The mapping process also reveals any holes in existing user data.

## Key Takeaways
- Empathy maps externalize knowledge about users to create shared understanding and aid decision making
- Traditional empathy maps have four quadrants: Says, Thinks, Does, and Feels
- Empathy mapping should be used throughout any UX process to establish common ground and prioritize user needs
- Aggregated empathy maps represent user segments and can synthesize themes for persona creation

## Applications
Empathy maps are used to:
- Categorize and make sense of qualitative research
- Discover gaps in current knowledge and identify needed research
- Create personas by aligning and grouping individual empathy maps
- Educate teammates about user attitudes and behaviors
- Collect data directly from users during interviews
- Align teams on shared understanding of user needs

## Process
1. Define scope and goals (user/persona, purpose)
2. Gather materials (whiteboard, sticky notes, markers)
3. Collect research (qualitative inputs: interviews, field studies, etc.)
4. Individually generate sticky notes for each quadrant
5. Converge to cluster and synthesize notes
6. Polish and plan (add detail, digitize, plan for future updates)
