# Laws of UX Overview

## Summary
Laws of UX is a collection of psychological principles and heuristics that inform user experience design, created by Jon Yablonski. These laws help designers understand how users perceive and interact with interfaces.

## Key Takeaways
- Based on psychological principles that govern human behavior
- Provides practical, evidence-based guidelines for interface design
- Includes well-known laws like Fitts's Law, Hick's Law, and Jakob's Law
- Each law includes explanation, examples, and related principles
- Continuously updated with new insights from cognitive psychology

## Origins/History
Created by Jon Yablonski as a reference for designers seeking to apply psychological principles to interface design. The collection has grown through community contributions and ongoing research in human-computer interaction.

## Applications
Laws of UX are applied by designers to:
- Predict user behavior and decision-making patterns
- Optimize interface layouts for efficiency and ease of use
- Reduce cognitive load through principled design choices
- Create interfaces that align with natural human tendencies
- Justify design decisions with psychological evidence
