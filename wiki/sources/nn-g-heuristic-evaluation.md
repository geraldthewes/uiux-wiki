# Nielsen Norman Group: Heuristic Evaluation

## Summary
Step-by-step instructions to systematically review your product to find potential usability and experience problems. Download a free heuristic evaluation template.

## Key Takeaways
- Heuristic evaluation identifies design problems by judging against usability guidelines (heuristics)
- Jakob Nielsen's 10 usability heuristics are recommended for general usability assessments
- The method is particularly helpful early in the design process and for stretching limited UX research budgets
- Heuristic evaluations complement but do not replace user research with actual participants
- Conducting heuristic evaluations helps develop strong UX instincts for catching common usability issues

## Process
1. **Choose and Train Your Team**: 3-5 evaluators independently evaluate the same interface
2. **Decide How to Document Evaluations**: Use workbook, spreadsheet, or digital whiteboard
3. **Set the Scope**: Narrow focus to one task, section, user group, or device type
4. **Evaluate Independently**: Familiarize with product, then identify heuristic violations
5. **Consolidate Identified Issues**: Affinity diagramming to synthesize findings
6. **Consider Exceptions**: Heuristics are guidelines; intentional violations require user research justification

## Applications
- Identifying glaring problems in interfaces (prototypes, physical products, games, VR, voice)
- Stretching limited UX research budgets
- Training UX practitioners to catch common usability issues
- Preparing for upcoming usability tests by identifying target design elements
- Developing UX instincts through practice
