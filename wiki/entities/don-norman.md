# Don Norman

## Summary
Don Norman is a cognitive scientist, usability engineer, and co-founder of the Nielsen Norman Group (NN/g). He is known for his advocacy of user-centered design and his influential books on design principles, including "The Design of Everyday Things."

## Key Takeaways
- Co-founder of Nielsen Norman Group with Jakob Nielsen
- Cognitive scientist and usability engineering pioneer
- Author of "The Design of Everyday Things" and other influential design books
- Advocate for user-centered design and cognitive aspects of usability
- Former VP of Apple's Advanced Technology Group
- Known for concepts like affordances, signifiers, and mapping in design

## Origins/History
Norman's career spans cognitive science, usability engineering, and design advocacy. He joined Apple in the 1990s before co-founding NN/g with Jakob Nielsen to advance the field of user experience.

## Applications
Norman's work is applied by UX professionals through:
- User-centered design methodologies
- Affordance and signifier concepts in interface design
- Cognitive psychology applications to UX
- Emotional design principles
- Design thinking and human-centered approaches
