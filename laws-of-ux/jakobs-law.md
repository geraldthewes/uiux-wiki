# Jakob's Law

Users spend most of their time on other sites. This means that users prefer your site to work the same way as all the other sites they already know.

## Overview
Also known as the "Law of Familiarity" or "Law of Internet UX," Jakob's Law states that users transfer expectations from one familiar product to another that appears similar.

## UX Applications
- Follow established platform conventions (iOS, Android, web)
- Use standard UI patterns and controls
- Maintain consistency with popular websites and applications
- Leverage users' existing mental models
- Avoid unnecessary innovation that breaks user expectations

## Design Implications
1. **Follow Platform Guidelines**: Adhere to Apple HIG, Material Design, etc.
2. **Use Standard Patterns**: Navigation menus, search bars, shopping carts, etc.
3. **Consistent Terminology**: Use familiar labels and icons
4. **Predictable Interactions**: Buttons should look and behave like buttons
5. **Progressive Enhancement**: Innovate while maintaining core familiarity
6. **User Testing**: Validate that your design meets user expectations

## Related Concepts
- **Mental Models**: Users' internal representations of how things work
- **Affordances**: Properties that show how an object can be used
- **Signifiers**: Indicators that communicate possible actions
- **Consistency and Standards**: NN/g Usability Heuristic #4
